A Family Built on Florida Landscapes Since 1981

Four Florida natives. One company. Forty-four years of knowing exactly what grows here — and why.

Not a Franchise. Not a Chain. A Family.

When Joe Alf founded Tropic Greenery in 1981, Brevard County was a different place — and so was the landscaping industry. There were no national lawn care franchises blanketing the Space Coast with generic programs. There were local operators who knew their territory and built their reputations one yard at a time. Tropic Greenery was built in that tradition, and 44 years later, it still operates the same way: a father and his children, all born and raised in Florida, caring for Brevard County landscapes with the specificity that only comes from living among them.


We are not a franchise. There is no corporate manual governing how we treat your property. Every decision — what to plant, how to fertilize, when to intervene — is made by the same family that answers the phone when you call. That directness and accountability is something no national company can replicate, no matter how many trucks they put on the road.

Meet the Team

Four people, four decades of combined Florida experience, and one shared commitment to doing this work the right way.

Joe Alf

Founder & Principal — Landscape Designer

Joe Alf founded Tropic Greenery in Melbourne in 1981 after years of hands-on work with Florida's most demanding plant material. His expertise in tropical and subtropical horticulture is exceptional by any measure: he served as past president of the Central Florida Palm Society, spent years as caretaker of the Florida Institute of Technology Botanical Garden — a living collection of rare tropical specimens — and was featured as a guest expert on PBS for his knowledge of Florida palm culture.

Joe's approach to landscape design is rooted in a principle he has held for four decades: the best landscape is one that belongs in its environment. That means designing with species that are adapted to Brevard County's sandy soil, salt exposure, and wet-dry seasonal cycle — not whatever looks fashionable in a magazine. He personally consults on every significant design project Tropic Greenery undertakes, bringing a depth of site-specific judgment that no software or catalog can replicate.

A lifelong Florida resident, Joe understands the Indian River Lagoon not as a regulatory constraint but as a shared resource he has watched change over the course of his career. That perspective informs every product decision, every planting plan, and every conversation he has with a client about what their landscape should do and how it should do it.

Benjamin Alf

Operations & Fertilization

Ben Alf grew up on job sites and in gardens — the kind of education that doesn't come from a classroom. As the operations lead for Tropic Greenery, Ben manages the day-to-day coordination of maintenance and fertilization crews across Brevard County, ensuring that every property on the schedule is visited by the right people with the right equipment at the right time.

Ben leads Tropic Greenery's fertilization and IPM program, applying the science-based diagnostic approach the company is known for. He is trained in Florida turf pest identification and integrated pest management protocols, and understands how Brevard County's seasonal weather patterns affect when and how treatments should be applied. When a client calls about a lawn problem, it's often Ben who shows up to look at it in person.

Dan Alf

Installation & Site Preparation

Dan Alf oversees Tropic Greenery's installation and land preparation work — the physical side of turning a design into a finished landscape. From land clearing and grading through planting and mulching, Dan manages the installation crews that execute Joe's designs in the field. His knowledge of site preparation is critical: proper grading and soil preparation in Florida's sandy substrate is what separates installations that establish well from ones that fail in the first season.

Dan also coordinates Tropic Greenery's hardscape work, ensuring that pavers, retaining walls, and irrigation installations are built to the same standard as the planting that surrounds them. He's the team member most likely to be driving equipment on a larger commercial project, and the one clients call when a job requires heavy lifting — literally and figuratively.

Janie Alf

Client Relations & Business Management

Janie Alf is the connective tissue of Tropic Greenery's operation — the person who ensures that what a client is promised is what they receive, that schedules are communicated clearly, and that questions get answered by someone who actually knows the answer. As the client-facing lead for the business, Janie manages estimates, scheduling, and ongoing client communication for both residential and commercial accounts.

Having grown up in the business, Janie understands every service Tropic Greenery offers at the ground level — not as a sales representative, but as someone who has watched her father and brothers do this work her entire life. That context makes her unusually effective at matching a client's actual needs to the right service, asking the right questions during a consultation, and setting realistic expectations about timeline, budget, and outcomes.

Janie is typically the first person a new client speaks to, and — because she stays involved through project completion — often the one they call with questions long after the initial job is done.

44 Years in Brevard County

A company built gradually — credential by credential, client by client.

1981

Tropic Greenery Founded

Joe Alf establishes Tropic Greenery in Melbourne, FL, offering landscape design and installation services to residential clients across Brevard County. The company begins with a focus on Florida-appropriate plant material at a time when most local landscaping relied on species poorly suited to the coastal climate.

1980s–90s

FIT Botanical Garden Stewardship

Joe Alf serves as caretaker of the Florida Institute of Technology Botanical Garden — a living collection of rare tropical and subtropical specimens in Melbourne. The role deepens his expertise in Florida's most demanding plant material and exposes him to horticultural practice at a level beyond ordinary residential landscaping.

1990s

Central Florida Palm Society — President

Joe Alf is elected president of the Central Florida Palm Society, a position reflecting his standing among Florida's most serious horticultural practitioners. His work in the palm community deepens Tropic Greenery's specialization in tropical species and connects the company to a network of growers, botanists, and collectors across the state.

2000s

PBS Feature & Service Expansion

Joe Alf appears as a featured guest on PBS, sharing expertise on Florida palm care and tropical horticulture to a public audience. Tropic Greenery expands its service offerings to include land clearing, grading, hardscape, and irrigation — becoming a full-service landscape contractor for residential and commercial clients.

2010s

Licensed Pest Control & Family Growth

Tropic Greenery obtains its Florida Lawn & Ornamental Pest Control Company license, formalizing a fertilization and IPM program that had been an informal part of the company's work for years. Ben, Dan, and Janie Alf join the business, bringing the next generation of the family into active daily operations.

2020s

Lagoon Friendly Designation & Keep Brevard Beautiful

Tropic Greenery is officially designated a Lagoon Friendly Lawn Care Contractor by the St. Johns River Water Management District, and earns Keep Brevard Beautiful certification — formalizing an environmental commitment the company has held since its founding. The business continues to operate as it always has: family-owned, crew-consistent, and rooted in the Brevard County community.

Today

44 Years and Still Growing

Tropic Greenery remains a family-operated business serving residential and commercial clients across all of Brevard County — from Titusville to Melbourne Beach. The same values that guided the company in 1981 guide every decision today: use the right plants, treat every property with care, and build relationships that last longer than any individual project.

Licenses, Certifications & Memberships

Credentials that reflect decades of commitment to professional standards and environmental responsibility.

FL Lawn & Ornamental Pest Control License

Licensed by the Florida Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services to apply pesticides and fertilizers for hire — a legal requirement many competitors in Brevard County ignore.

Lagoon Friendly Lawn Care Contractor

Florida Plant Bonded Dealer

Certified by the state of Florida as a bonded plant dealer — ensuring plant material sold and installed meets Florida nursery standards for health, labeling, and origin documentation.

Keep Brevard Beautiful Certified

Recognized by Keep Brevard Beautiful for operating in a manner consistent with the organization's environmental stewardship standards for Brevard County businesses.

Central Florida Palm Society — Past President

Joe Alf served as president of the Central Florida Palm Society, a membership organization for Florida's most serious palm and tropical plant enthusiasts, growers, and professionals.

Licensed & Fully Insured

Tropic Greenery carries full general liability and workers' compensation insurance, protecting every client and every crew member on every job — a basic standard too many competitors skip.
